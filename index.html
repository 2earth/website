I'm an engineer working in Glasgow, Scotland. This personal blog is a collection of my writing on whatever I find interesting. Underpinning this all are a few fundamental perspectives - I think "we" have an urgent imperative but also a great opportunity as engineers, designers...people, to improve the world (and stop waste, pollution, poverty, emissions - etc). I'm a proponent of the circular economy (as part of the solution), an advocate for standards and open collaboration and typically critical of proprietary, capitalistic things.

I've re-engineered many commercial light fittings ("luminaires") so they can be remanufactured not recycled; I've had a key role in drafting BS 8887-221:2025 (a standard which describes the general process for remanufacturing luminaires). Previously I helped design a microplastic filter for washing machines and now have some patents relating to filtration and filter regeneration (I'm ambivalent about patents).

Other than work I am keen on climbing hard bouldering problems, sketching buildings and reading. My main interest is currently early modernist architecture and design. Please recommend me a book.
